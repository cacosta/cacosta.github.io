About Me

I loved to code the minute I made a turtle move across the screen with GOTO statements in the 7th grade. Without any female role models to encourage me to focus on computers, my love of code remained dormant while I went on to pursue a professional career in Dance and Musical Theater. I eventually returned to my love of technology and pursued a B.S. in Computer Science in 2003. I have since developed a career in Web Development spanning over 10 years and currently part of an innovative and creative team at The New York Times.

I have a desire to inspire and encourage women of all ages and backgrounds to pursue technology with the hope of closing the gender gap that exists in STEM occupations and education which led me to become a teacher and member of the Leadership Team at Girl Develop It, a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization that teaches and encourages women to learn technology one line of code at a time. In addition, I am passionate about developing technology that promotes social and cultural change, lending my skills on open source projects with organizations such as Code for America.

When I'm not coding, you can find me dabbling in photography, teaching, dancing, tinkering, or bending myself into a pretzel on a yoga mat.

Berklee Online Site Refresh

Role: Front End Developer

I am proud to have been one of the key developers involved with rebuilding the entire Berklee Online site from concept to launch, collaborating directly with our Creative Director, Designer, Video and Marketing teams to ensure continuity, style guide considerations, and a focus on user interaction.

Responsibilities included integrating Twitter Bootstrap from the ground up, building interactive page layouts in Ruby on Rails using jQuery, incorporating modern methodologies in responsive design, and making pivotal decisions regarding which specific front end solutions were right for the project, for example, my decision to utilize the Brightcove API to deliver video content sitewide.

Used: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Ruby on Rails, Haml, Twitter Bootstrap, LESS

Custom Dynamic Video Players for Berklee Online

Role: Lead Front End Developer

Tasked with designing, prototyping, and developing custom JavaScript-based video players that dynamically display branded video content served through Brightcove across the site using JQuery, JSON, and the Brightcove API. Responsible for all research and decision-making with regards to video player integration displayed across the entire Berklee Online domain.

Due to the time-sensitive nature of this project and my limited knowledge of Ruby on Rails at the time, I initially built the players with JQuery but eventually felt they could perform better and could be better maintained internally if the API calls were made within our Ruby on Rails infrastructure. I become the project lead on this initiative, working directly with our lead Ruby on Rails Developer to refactor the code in accordance with the current best practices at the time.

Used: HTML, CSS, JQuery, Brightcove API, Ruby on Rails, Haml
